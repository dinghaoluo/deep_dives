**Where it ended:** Designing a literary-visual website about the politics of the twelve-tone grid, modelled on Swinehart's *Infinite Digest*

---

## The Thread

The session opened with Busoni's case against equal temperament as a collective hallucination — and quickly became an excavation of *why* twelve tones, not some other number. The key insight was that the Pythagorean method of stacking pure fifths (×3/2, drop an octave if needed) generates notes organically from acoustic physics, and twelve applications nearly-but-not-quite closes the loop back to the starting pitch. That failure — the Pythagorean comma — is not an engineering error but an irrationality baked into the relationship between the octave (2:1) and the fifth (3:2): log₂(3/2) is transcendental, and twelve is simply the smallest denominator that approximates it well (as a continued-fraction convergent). Equal temperament solves the comma by distributing it uniformly across all twelve steps — a compromise that makes every key equally slightly wrong, discovered simultaneously by Zhu Zaiyu in Ming dynasty China (1584) and Simon Stevin in the Low Countries. From there the session moved into the politics of infrastructure: equal temperament won not on acoustic grounds but because the piano got built and exported, and the Burkean/Chesterton's Fence logic of the tonal grid was entangled with ecclesiastical and state power from at least the medieval organ. The session concluded with a project proposal — a literary-visualisation website about the comma problem and its history, with *Infinite Digest* as the aesthetic benchmark.

---

## Key Nodes

- **Pythagorean comma** — the gap produced by stacking 12 pure fifths: (3/2)^12 ≠ 2^7, difference ≈ 23.46 cents. The irrationality of log₂(3/2) means no finite stack of fifths ever closes cleanly.
- **Continued fraction convergents of log₂(3/2)** — the mathematical reason 12 is special: it is the smallest denominator q such that q × log₂(3/2) is close to an integer. The next meaningful improvement requires 41 tones. Pentatonic scale (5 tones) is the prior convergent.
- **Zhu Zaiyu (朱載堉), 1584** — Ming dynasty prince-mathematician who computed the twelfth root of 2 to 25 decimal places by hand, motivated by cosmological-calendrical concerns (pitch-pipe standardisation linking music to celestial cycles). Exact contemporary of Simon Stevin's independent European solution.
- **Well temperament vs. equal temperament** — well temperament distributes the comma *unevenly*, giving different keys different emotional characters that Bach exploited. Equal temperament irons all keys to identical colour, arguably flattening compositional palette.
- **Busoni's *Entwurf* (1907/1913)** — proposed dissolving the twelve-tone grid entirely in favour of continuous pitch; called for electrical instruments to realise it. Pfitzner's response ("aesthetic Bolshevism") framed tonal reform as political threat.
- **Telharmonium (Cahill, 1897)** — 200-ton electrical instrument transmitting music over telephone wires; precursor to what Busoni imagined. Theremin (Lev Termen, 1920) arrived independently, demonstrated to Lenin.
- **Diabolus in musica** — the tritone, formally prohibited in medieval sacred composition. The grid's entanglement with ecclesiastical power from the start.
- **Chesterton's Fence** — the principle that you must understand why a constraint exists before removing it; applied here to equal temperament's role as collaborative infrastructure for MIDI, notation, ensemble playing.
- **Spidermilk (Mercury Tree)** — fully microtonal prog album; Dinghao's description of the listening experience as "notes that *should have been* xx" — the twelve-tone grid persisting as implicit ghost even when overturned.
- **Christian Swinehart / *Infinite Digest*** — benchmark literary-visualisation project: full-bleed dark aesthetic, italic serif display type, visualisations that emerge *through* scrolled prose rather than replacing it, data made structurally inseparable from narrative.

## Dinghao's Best Contributions

- Identifying immediately that the comma arises from stacking operations, then asking *why 12* specifically — the right question that unlocked the continued-fraction explanation.
- "The notes feel like 'they should have been xx' — a new spectrum. Instead of expanding the 12-tone system, it may have been to fundamentally challenge it, and overturn the idea that 12-tone is the truth." — the sharpest phenomenological account of microtonal listening in the session.
- The Burkean/bad-code parallel: "if it's not broke, don't fix it... that's how we have mountains of bad code that cannot get cleaned up" — productive deflation that led to the Chesterton's Fence refinement.
- Noting that Zhu Zaiyu's simultaneous discovery makes the "Western music history" framing untenable: "It seems to be a less clear split than just 'western' vs 'the world'."
