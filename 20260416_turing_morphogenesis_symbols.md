# Session Notes — 16 Apr 2026

**Aperture:** Turing's 1952 morphogenesis paper and the trajectory from codebreaking to computation to pattern formation
**Where it ended:** The ontological status of emergent patterns and whether symbol manipulation requires a manipulator — with complexity theory parked as a major thread for next session

---

## The Thread

The session opened on Turing's "Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" and quickly branched into neuroscience, where the activator-inhibitor logic maps directly onto centre-surround receptive fields (ON-centre, OFF-surround; difference of Gaussians / Mexican hat) and plausibly onto grid cell formation in entorhinal cortex. The philosophical weight shifted toward the representational status of emergent patterns — the Game of Life glider as a test case — and the distinction between formal symbols (syntactic tokens, meaning-agnostic), semantic symbols (signs with intentional content), and morphogens (physical quantities, no representational content whatsoever). This led to Searle's Chinese Room, dispatched via the systems reply, and then to the harder question of whether formal symbol manipulation requires intentions — resolved tentatively through the notion of *crystallised intentionality*, with AI authorship as the pressure point. The session closed on Turing's trajectory as a progression toward fundamentals, with a parked tension about whether morphogenesis also represents a reckoning with the limits of his own computational paradigm.

---

## Key Nodes

- **Turing reaction-diffusion (1952):** Activator-inhibitor dynamics on an undifferentiated field; symmetry breaking from random fluctuation; confirmed in mouse hair follicles 2012. Starting point for the session.
- **Mexican hat / difference of Gaussians:** Centre-surround receptive field structure in retina and visual cortex; structurally homologous to Turing's local excitation / lateral inhibition. Dinghao's entry point.
- **Grid cells (entorhinal cortex):** Hexagonal firing fields plausibly generated by Turing-type dynamics; Dinghao's intuition, well-founded in the literature.
- **Game of Life glider:** A persistent pattern produced by local rules with no blueprint. Key test case for the representational status of emergent structure. Conclusion: the glider is not a representation — it has no external referent and points nowhere. Representation requires a modeller who treats it as a handle.
- **Three-way symbol taxonomy:** Morphogens (physical dynamics, no representation) / formal symbols (syntactic tokens, meaning-agnostic) / semantic symbols (intentional content, interpreter-constituted). Clarifies Searle's equivocations and the Turing machine's deliberate meaning-agnosticism.
- **Searle's Chinese Room:** Rejected via the systems reply — the room as a whole does what understanding Chinese requires. Searle's rebuttal (homunculus gets bigger) question-begs intentionality. Genuine lacuna: the static lookup table doesn't *learn*; plasticity may be the missing element, not intentionality per se.
- **Crystallised intentionality:** Programmer's intentions fossilised into transition rules; the engine behaves as if it has goals because someone who had goals encoded them. Sharp formulation from this session.
- **AI authorship and sorites:** If AI writes code that shapes future training, at what point does crystallised intentionality cease to be traceable to a human source? No clean threshold — sorites all the way down.
- **Turing's trajectory:** Codebreaking (semantic symbol use) → Turing machine (strip meaning out entirely) → morphogenesis (pre-symbolic dynamics). Read as progression toward fundamentals; tension noted — morphogenesis also resists his own computational paradigm.

## Dinghao's Best Contributions

- **Glider as rule-selector, not representation:** "The glider decides the rule. Unless one has a prior understanding of how the rule would affect the pattern, and specifically applies a certain glider value to get that pattern, one should not think of the glider as representation itself." Sharp and correct — closes the representational status question cleanly.
- **Morphogens are not symbols:** Drew the line precisely — morphogens follow spontaneous organisational rules and lack the relational structure that makes something a symbol. The distinction between network dynamics (his neuroscience frame) and field differentiation (Turing's embryological frame) also his observation.
- **Crystallised intentionality as distilled intention:** Extended the chess-engine case to RL and AI training — intentionality distilled into engines by Anthropic, but traceable. Then pressed the fracture point: AI-written code, self-sustaining systems, the sorites of intentional origin.
- **Turing's trajectory as progression, not retreat:** "Getting closer to bare-bone truths" — life emerged from exactly such symbol-less, manipulator-less dynamics. Not too generous; precisely framed.
